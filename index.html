Summary

PhD candidate in Zoology (developmental biology) specialising in computational analysis of complex biological datasets to understand how developmental environments shape adult phenotype and performance. I combine mechanistic experimental measurements with high-resolution time-series data and build reproducible pipelines for data cleaning, modelling, and reporting.

I’m particularly drawn to roles that involve building end-to-end analytical pipelines and applying machine learning thoughtfully (with careful validation and interpretability) to complex biological questions.

Key strengths

  • Computational biology & modelling: statistical modelling, model comparison, and diagnostics in R; translating biological questions into quantitative frameworks.
  • Machine learning & data engineering mindset: feature construction from time-series/environmental data; Python/SQL for wrangling and structured data handling.
  • Mechanistic + predictive integration: linking trait assays (reflectance spectroscopy, heating-rate assays) with fine-scale environmental drivers to explain and predict phenotype variation.
  • Reproducibility & communication: well-documented code, versioned workflows, clear scientific writing; comfortable collaborating across wet–dry teams.

Education

Stellenbosch University — PhD Candidate, Zoology (Developmental and Thermal Biology)

Jan 2023 – Jul 2026

Project: Developmental plasticity of melanisation in insects: effects on thermoregulation
Supervisor: Prof. Susana Clusella-Trullas

  • Investigate how larval developmental temperature shapes adult melanisation, cuticular reflectance, and thermal performance in the invasive ladybeetle Harmonia axyridis.
  • Built integrated datasets combining multi-year microclimate logger time series (15-minute resolution) with phenotypic and physiological measurements.
  • Designed and executed controlled experiments using visible and near-infrared light sources approximating solar spectra to test how melanisation and cuticle optics influence heating rates and insect fitness.
  • Analysed reflectance spectra across cuticle regions and linked spectral variation to warming dynamics and developmental history.
